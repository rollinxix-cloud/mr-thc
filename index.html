Ganja in Nepal: A Rich History

For centuries, cannabis has played a massive role in Nepal’s culture, Ayurvedic medicine, and religious festivals like Maha Shivaratri. From the wild hills of Rolpa to the famous 'Freak Street' era of the 1970s, Nepal was a global hub for pure, high-quality ganja before foreign pressure changed the landscape.

The Narcotic Drugs (Control) Act, 2033 (1976)

Under strict international pressure, Nepal passed the Drug Act of 2033, enforcing a blanket ban on cultivation, sale, and possession. However, things are shifting. Recent discussions in parliament and bills introduced by lawmakers are pushing for limited medical exceptions, wild ganja utilization, and research allowances.

The Benefits: Why Reform Matters

  • Economic Promise: High-quality hemp export, job creation, and crop diversification for local farmers.
  • Medical Value: Natural pain management, traditional Ayurvedic applications, and epilepsy/chemotherapy relief.
  • Industrial Potential: Eco-friendly clothing, building materials, and bioplastics from hemp fibers.
